Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly used to take action or augment human decision-making, yet most approaches rely on the model's parametric knowledge, possibly supplemented with fixed data or tools. This paradigm breaks down for novel domains or sophisticated queries that are not practically answerable from general prior knowledge. Instead, active experimentation may be needed to gather targeted, query-specific data in the domain.

We introduce Hierarchical Experimentalist Agents (HeXA), a novel in-context, experiment-centric reasoning approach that (1) iteratively designs and refines query-relevant experiments; (2) incrementally learns a library of reusable, composable skills that accelerate experimentation within and across tasks; and (3) integrates experimental data to effectively take actions or answer queries. HeXA self-improves in-context while co-evolving skills and strategies from interaction rewards and feedback — all without access to offline data, an optimal oracle, or teacher guidance.

On InterPhyre, our new 2D procedural physics benchmark, HeXA reaches 64% success on the hardest level with Claude Sonnet 4.6, compared to 8% for ReAct and 22% for Reflexion. Skill banks learned on easier levels transfer to harder unseen levels with no further experimentation, yielding up to +36 pp from transferred skills alone.

The HeXA Framework

01

Active Experimentation

An actor LLM interacts with a simulator through a tool-call interface, deliberately probing the environment with state-inspection, partial rollouts, and full simulations to gather query-relevant evidence.

02

Skill Distillation

An evolver LLM reads batches of successful and failed trajectories, contrasts high- against low-reward episodes, and distills them into compact natural-language skills and common-mistake records.

03

Skill Evolution & Transfer

A reward-weighted retriever injects top-confidence skills into each new episode. Skills are merged, revised, or pruned across rounds — and re-grounded onto structurally related target tasks with no target-level data.

InterPhyre Benchmark

We release InterPhyre, an extensible 2D procedural physics environment designed to make hypothesis generation, active experimentation, and reusable skill learning explicit and measurable. It exposes a structured tool-call interface for scene inspection, partial simulation, contact logs, and per-level geometric analysis, alongside an intervention API with snapshot/restore semantics for paired counterfactual analysis.

  • 25 levels, 10,000 oracle-certified seeds each — 250,000 pre-validated task instances.
  • Programmatic interventions for paired counterfactual trajectories from a shared physical prefix.
  • Independent physics axes — vary gravity, friction, restitution without changing scene topology.
  • Gymnasium & tool-call interfaces share one simulator backend.
